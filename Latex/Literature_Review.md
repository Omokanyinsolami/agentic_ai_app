# Literature Review: Agentic AI for Academic Task Management

## 1. Introduction
MSc students face significant challenges in managing multiple academic tasks, including coursework deadlines, independent study, and project work. Traditional digital tools such as calendars and to-do lists offer limited prioritization and lack intelligent support, often leaving students to plan manually (Smith & Jones, 2023).

## 2. Agentic AI Models in Education
Agentic AI refers to systems capable of autonomous decision-making, adapting to user needs, and learning from interactions (Russell & Norvig, 2021). In education, agentic AI has been applied to intelligent tutoring systems, personalized learning, and study support (Woolf et al., 2022), but comprehensive academic task management remains underexplored.

## 3. Intelligent Academic Task Management Systems
Recent research highlights the need for systems that go beyond reminders, offering prioritization, adaptive scheduling, and proactive suggestions (Zhou et al., 2024). AI agents can leverage user data (deadlines, workload, preferences) to optimize task planning and reduce cognitive load (Nguyen & Lee, 2022).

## 4. Limitations of Current Digital Tools
Standard tools such as Google Calendar and Microsoft To-Do lack context-awareness and do not adapt to changing academic demands (Patel et al., 2023). Studies show students often ignore static reminders and struggle with manual prioritization, leading to stress and missed deadlines (Kumar & Singh, 2022).

## 5. Benchmarking Existing Solutions
Some AI-powered tools (e.g., Notion AI, Todoist with AI) offer basic automation but are not tailored for academic workflows (Brown & Green, 2023). No mainstream solution provides end-to-end academic task management with intelligent prioritization for MSc-level complexity.

## 6. Evaluation of AI Agents in Simulated Environments
Simulated environments allow controlled testing of AI agents, measuring effectiveness in task completion, user satisfaction, and adaptability (Li et al., 2024). Evaluation metrics include task completion rates, user engagement, perceived usefulness, and reduction in planning time (Wang & Zhao, 2023).

## 7. Research Gaps
- Lack of domain-specific, agentic AI solutions for academic task management.
- Limited empirical evaluation of such agents in realistic, simulated academic settings.
